Mockingbird is a Google Chrome extension for desktop Twitter that changes the headings and names of key functions into their literal intentions. Inspired by Jenny Odell's ideas on the attention economy, it exposes how social media pushes users to waste time and be emotionally invested. Each trigger and label was chosen with user interaction in mind.

Purpose—Student Project
Type—Chrome Extension
Languages—HTML, Javascript
Notes—No longer available on the Chrome store as of 2021

WATCH DEMO↓

LOAD

Upon loading Twitter, there is a delay before the headings change. It serves as an alarm to awaken obsessive Twitter users who are oblivious to the amount of energy they pour into social media. Ironically, it draws attention to the altered labels and invokes a realisation of the platform's motives, where they grab our attention to force users to stay.


SCROLL

When the user scrolls down on the home page, it triggers the 'Wormhole' that sucks you into endless scrolling. The same thing occurs where the headings on the notifications, bookmarks and lists pages are changed upon scroll.


ON CLICK/TYPING

The large Tweet button on the menu remains the same to tease and remind users of what used to be. But as the button is clicked on and the modal box appears, the placeholder text switches. As the user types, so does the Tweet button. This impact places emphasis on the change as it is visible. Similarly on the 'Messages' page, a chat needs to be clicked on to cause the title change. These interactions ask users to think twice before engaging with the platform, in hopes of deterring them from frequent usage and lurking.
