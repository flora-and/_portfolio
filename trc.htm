The Reading Club↗ is an interactive website that explores the connection between the brain and the digital. Inspired by the aesthetics of Net Art, it addresses and critiques the way the rapid processes of the Internet have shifted our reading habits.

Purpose—Student Project
Type—Desktop Website
Languages—HTML, 3D CSS, Javascript
Notes—Optimised for Google Chrome (1920x1080), no longer updated as of 2021
Visit—Here↗

It introduces a questionnaire about an imaginary book club that users must complete in order to successfully join the online community. These questions take the form of 3D rectangular prisms, which mimic advertisements. They simulate the inconvenience of pop up ads by producing more windows when the user selects the incorrect answer.

As the number of windows accumulate, so does the amount of text. As a result, these distractions challenge the reading comprehension and concentration of the user. This reflects our expectations and routine practices in reading that have sped up to match the fast-paced mechanisms of the web and its disturbing format.
